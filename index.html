BIOGRAFI

Soekarno

The First President of Indonesia

Here's a time line of Soekarno life:

  • 6 June 1901 - Born in Surabaya
  • 1912 - graduating from a native primary school, and he was sent to the Europeesche Lagere School (a Dutch primary school) in Mojokerto.
  • 1916 - Soekarno went to a Hogere Burgerschool (a Dutch type higher level secondary school) in Surabaya, where he met Tjokroaminoto, a nationalist and founder of Sarekat Islam.
  • 1920 - Soekarno married Tjokroaminoto's daughter Siti Oetari.
  • 1921 - he began to study civil engineering (with focusing on architecture) at the Technische Hoogeschool te Bandoeng (Bandoeng Institute of Technology)
  • 1926 - he obtained an Ingenieur degree (abbreviated as "Ir.", a Dutch type engineer's degree).
  • 1923 - Soekarno divorced Siti Oetari to marry Inggit (who also divorced her husband Sanoesi). Soekarno later divorced Inggit and married Fatmawati.
  • 1926 - Soekarno and his university friend Anwari established the architectural firm Soekarno & Anwari in Bandung, which provided planning and contractor services
  • 4 July 1927 - Soekarno with his friends from the Algemeene Studieclub established a pro-independence party, the Indonesian National Party (PNI), of which Soekarno was elected the first leader.
  • 29 December 1929 - Soekarno and other key PNI leaders were arrested by Dutch colonial authorities in a series of raids throughout Java
  • 31 December 1931 - Soekarno was released early and he had become a popular hero widely known throughout Indonesia.
  • 28 July 1932 - Soekarno chose to become the head of Partindo
  • mid-1933 - Soekarno published a series of writings titled Mentjapai Indonesia Merdeka ("To Attain Independent Indonesia").
  • 1 August 1933 - he was arrested by Dutch police while visiting fellow nationalist Mohammad Hoesni Thamrin in Jakarta
  • 1934 - Soekarno was shipped, along with his family (including Inggit Garnasih), to the remote town of Ende, on the island of Flores.
  • February 1938 - Dutch authorities decided to move Soekarno and his family to Bencoolen (now Bengkulu) on the western coast of Sumatra
  • February 1942 - Imperial Japan invaded the Dutch East Indies quickly defeating Dutch forces who marched, bussed and trucked Sukarno and his entourage three hundred kilometres from Bengkulu to Padang, Sumatra.
  • July 1942 - Soekarno was sent back to Jakarta, where he re-united with other nationalist leaders recently released by the Japanese, including Mohammad Hatta.
  • March 1943 - the Japanese formed a new organisation called Poesat Tenaga Rakjat (POETERA/ Center of People's Power) under Soekarno, Hatta, Ki Hadjar Dewantara, and KH Mas Mansjoer.
  • 10 November 1943 - Soekarno and Hatta were sent on a 17-day tour of Japan, where they were decorated by Emperor Hirohito and wined and dined in the house of Prime Minister Hideki Tojo in Tokyo
  • 1 June 1945 - he introduced these five principles, known as pancasila, during the joint session of the BPUPK held in the former Volksraad Building (now called Gedung Pancasila).
  • 16 August 1945 - the three youth leaders, impatient with Soekarno's indecision, kidnapped him from his house and brought him to a small house in Rengasdengklok, Karawang, owned by a Chinese family and occupied by PETA.
  • 17 August 1945 - Soekarno returned to his house at Jalan Pegangsaan Timur No. 56, where Mohammad Hatta joined him. Throughout the morning, impromptu leaflets printed by PETA and youth elements informed the population of the impending proclamation. Finally, at 10 am, Soekarno and Hatta stepped to the front porch, where Soekarno declared the independence of the Republic of Indonesia in front of a crowd of 500 people.

This country, the Republic of Indonesia, does not belong to any group, nor to any religion, nor to any ethnic group, nor to any group with customs and traditions, but the property of all of us from Sabang to Merauke!

-- Ir. Soekarno
